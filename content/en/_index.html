Degrowth advocates for a move away from the traditional emphasis on economic growth as a primary indicator of success and well-being.

The pursuit of endless economic growth leads to environmental degradation, resource depletion and social inequality. Degrowth proposes that we focus on alternative indicators of well-being, such as quality of life, social justice, and environmental sustainability.

The degrowth movement suggests rethinking the current economic system, challenging the notion that continuous growth is essential, and exploring alternative models that prioritise human well-being and ecological sustainability. This will involve changes in consumption patterns, work structures, and societal values to create a more resilient and equitable society.
